nanometer and below accounted for 54% of wafer revenue.

technology will use FinFET transistor structure to deliver the best technology maturity, performance and cost. Our
N3 technology development is on track with good progress. N3 risk production is scheduled in 2021 and volume
production is targeted in second half of 2022. We have already demonstrated 256-megabit SRAM functionality.
N3 logic test chip is fully functional with yield ahead of plan.

technology in both PPA and transistor technology when it is introduced, which will further extend our leadership
position well into the future.
Finally, let me talk about our US fab plan. On May 15, we announced our intention to build an advanced
semiconductor fab in the US. We have received the commitment to support this project from both the US Federal
Government and the State of Arizona. We are working closely with them, as well as our supply chain partners to
build an effective supply chain and makeup the cost gap. This fab will start with 5-nanometer technology with
20,000 wafer per month capacity. Production is targeted to begin in 2024. US fab will enable TSMC to expand our
